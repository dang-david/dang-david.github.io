Ranked Ladder & Esports

Climb from Iron to Challenger and explore one of the biggest esports scenes in the world.

Ranked Tiers & Progression

The Ranked system places players in skill tiers. From lowest to highest, the tiers are Iron, Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, Emerald, Diamond, Master, Grandmaster, and Challenger. Most tiers up to Diamond are divided into four divisions (IV to I).

After each Ranked game, you gain or lose League Points (LP) based on whether you win or lose, as well as your hidden matchmaking rating (MMR). Reaching 100 LP in a division promotes you upward, while falling too low can demote you. Each split and season brings soft resets and rewards like icons, borders, and skins for reaching certain ranks.

Competitive Esports Scene

League of Legends has a massive professional esports ecosystem. Regional leagues such as the LCK (Korea), LPL (China), LEC (Europe), and LCS (North America) run seasonal splits where teams compete for championship titles and spots at international events.

The two biggest global tournaments are the Mid-Season Invitational (MSI) and the World Championship (Worlds). Worlds, held at the end of each year, brings together the best teams from every region to play on stage in front of huge live crowds and millions of viewers online.

Improving at Ranked

To climb the ladder, it helps to specialize in one role and a small pool of champions. This lets you focus on learning matchups, wave management, and macro decisions instead of constantly swapping picks. Reviewing your own replays, tracking your deaths, and noticing when you misplay around objectives are simple habits that can dramatically improve your win rate over time.

There are many tools that can help you learn, such as official champion spotlights, community guides, and stat-tracking websites. Using these resources to understand item builds, rune choices, and power spikes will make each game feel more deliberate and less random.
