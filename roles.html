Roles in League of Legends

Top, Jungle, Mid, ADC, and Support — what each position contributes to the team.

Role Overview

A standard team in League of Legends is made up of five roles: Top, Jungle, Mid, ADC, and Support. Each role plays in a different part of the map and has a specific job, from tanking damage on the front line to dealing consistent DPS or providing crowd control and vision. Understanding your role’s responsibilities is key to playing well with your team.

Top Lane

Top laners usually play bruisers or tanks in a long solo lane. Their early game focuses on surviving, farming, and winning 1v1 or 1v2 skirmishes. Later on, they become a front-liner who can either start fights or apply pressure by split-pushing in a side lane.

Jungle

Junglers clear neutral monster camps and look for opportunities to gank lanes. They control major objectives like Dragon, Rift Herald, and Baron Nashor using the Smite summoner spell. A good jungler tracks the enemy jungler, sets up vision, and helps their laners snowball an early lead.

Mid Lane

Mid laners often play mages or assassins. Because mid is in the center of the map, they can push their wave and roam to help side lanes or contest river objectives. Mid champions typically provide high burst damage, crowd control, or important team utility.

ADC (Bot Carry)

The ADC, or Attack Damage Carry, plays in the bottom lane and focuses on ranged damage. Early in the game they are vulnerable and rely on their support, but with enough items they become one of the highest damage threats on the team. Their main job is to stay safe while dealing continuous damage to enemies and objectives.

Support

Supports lane with the ADC and focus on vision, utility, and peel. They buy support items that generate gold, place wards to control vision, and use crowd control or shielding to protect their carries. A strong support keeps the team safe and sets up winning fights.

Role Comparison at a Glance

Top and Jungle usually front-line and control side-lane pressure, while Mid and ADC provide the majority of a team’s damage. Support ties the composition together by enabling plays, protecting allies, and controlling vision. When each role understands its job, the team can coordinate around objectives and win games much more consistently.
